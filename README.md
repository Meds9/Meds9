## MIND Framework

**Modeling Influence, Neutralizing Deception**

HPL (Human Programming Language) formalized how influence operations work by expressing them as structured pseudocode -- triggers, state transitions, behavioral loops, and targeting logic. It made the mechanics of persuasion readable and analyzable.

MIND builds on that foundation. If influence can be modeled as code, defense can be engineered as code too.

### What it does

- **Analyzes content** across 6 detection layers -- source verification, content analysis, behavioral patterns, network analysis, historical context, and cross-referencing
- **Produces a Manipulation Probability Score** (MPS) from 0 to 1, providing a quantified assessment of influence indicators
- **Simulates threat scenarios** with 8 attack vectors and 6 defense layers, allowing researchers and educators to study influence dynamics in a controlled environment
- **Includes 100 educational examples** across 5 modules, each pairing a threat model with its defensive counterpart

### Why it exists

Understanding influence operations is a literacy problem. The methods are well-documented in academic research, but the tools to study them interactively have been limited. MIND provides a structured, open-source environment for learning how influence works and how to build defenses against it.

The goal is informed, independent thinking -- giving people the tools to evaluate what they encounter and draw their own conclusions.
